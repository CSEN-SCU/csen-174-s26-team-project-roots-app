Positioning snapshot

  • For

    People who want to compile and share ideas with friends across trips, projects, and routes that link together—but stay stuck in inaction from doom-saving.

  • Who struggle with

    Bridging digital inspiration (Reels, TikToks) to real-world follow-through, because manual planning, research, and compilation add friction right when motivation is highest.

  • The Roots app is

    A context-aware personal planner that automatically turns saved social content into actionable calendar events and step-by-step project roadmaps.

  • Unlike

    Traditional calendars or save folders that behave like static archives, Roots proactively extracts hidden metadata, logistics, and instructional steps from video so progress does not die in the research phase.

  • Powered by

    Multimodal content extraction: combined analysis of video, audio, and captions to surface locations, dates, and technical instructions—even when creators never drop a pin or a clean address.

Why saves go cold

The problem in context

Feeds reward the next swipe; saves reward forgetting. Built-in location tags help sometimes, but they are unreliable—and buried saves make returning costly. When details are missing, you inherit the whole research job, and many ideas never survive it.

Inspiration without a bridge

Social platforms surface endless trips and projects, yet offer little structure for working toward them as a group or over time.

Where existing tools stop

Saves and light location metadata help in theory, but fast-scrolling culture and clunky retrieval mean most content never becomes a plan you can execute.

The cost of “figure it out yourself”

When posts omit logistics, hours, or routes, the mental load lands on you—so ideas stall at research instead of becoming dates on a calendar or steps on a board.

How AI makes Roots possible

From a reel to reality—without retyping the internet

Multimodal extraction reads what is said, shown, and heard—not only the caption—so Roots can infer places, timing cues, and how-to detail, then fold them into schedules and roadmaps that respect real-world constraints like hours of operation and transit.

Calendar events that write themselves

Promising saves graduate into dated, place-aware commitments you can share—so “someday” becomes blocked time and clearer next steps.

Roadmaps, not piles of links

Instructional steps and dependencies surface as ordered work—ideal for projects and multi-stop routes you want to link between friends.

Logistics surfaced early

Transportation questions, opening hours, and sequencing that used to require tab-hopping get pulled forward so execution feels lighter than the save.

“Without AI, Roots would be you manually re-entering everything into a calendar or notes app— researching every detail by hand. The product’s edge is how fast a reel can become reality.”
